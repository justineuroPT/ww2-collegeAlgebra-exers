    7. local/Library/Utah/Intermediate_Algebra/set8_Quadratic_Equations_Functions_and_Inequalities/s8p11.pg.

    Complete the following equations. Your answers will be algebraic expressions.
    \((a+b i)(a-b i)=\) \(+\)
    \(i\)
    \((a+b i)^2-(a-b i)^2=\) \(+\)
    \(i\)
    Answer 1.
    \(a^{2}+b^{2}\)
    Answer 2.
    \(0\)
    Answer 3.
    \(0\)
    Answer 4.
    \(4ab\)
    Solution.
    Solution: The product of a complex number and its conjugate complex equals the sum of the squares of the real and imaginary parts. Of course we can also see this directly:
    \begin{equation*} (a+bi)(a-bi) = a^2 +abi-abi-b^2i^2 = a^2 + b^2 \end{equation*}
    For the second part of the problem, note that
    \begin{equation*} (a+bi)^2 = a^2-b^2 + 2abi. \end{equation*}
    By replacing \(b\) with \(-b\) in this equation we obtain
    \begin{equation*} (a+bi)^2 = a^2-b^2 - 2abi. \end{equation*}
    Notice only the imaginary part of the result changes (from positive to negative). Hence
    \begin{equation*} (a+bi)^2 - (a-bi)^2 = 4abi. \end{equation*}

    8. local/Library/Utah/Intermediate_Algebra/set8_Quadratic_Equations_Functions_and_Inequalities/s8p10.pg.

    Complete the following equation. Your answers will be algebraic expressions.

    Answer 2.

    Solution.
    Solution: We use the result of the preceding problem and obtain:
    \begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rcl} (a+bi)^3 \amp =\amp (a+bi)^2 \times (a+bi) \\ \amp =\amp \left(\left(a^2-b^2\right) + 2abi\right)\times (a+bi) \\ \amp =\amp a\left(a^2-b^2\right) + 2a^2bi +b \left(a^2-b^2\right)i+2ab^2i^2 \\ \amp =\amp a^3-ab^2 -2ab^2 +\left(2a^2b +a^2b -b^3\right)i \\ \amp =\amp \left(a^3-3ab^2\right) +\left(3a^2b -b^3\right)i \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
