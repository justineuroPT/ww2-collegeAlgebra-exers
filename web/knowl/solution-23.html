Solution 17.8.1.

To find the equation of a line, you need a point and a slope. To find the slope between the 2 points, use the slope formula:
M = (Y2 - Y1)/(X2 - X1)
Then to find the equation, Use the slope-intercept form of a line, y = Mx + b. Plug the point in for x and y, and plug M in from the previous formula, and solve for b (y - intercept). Then rewrite the equation using the variables x and y, and the value calculated for M and b.
