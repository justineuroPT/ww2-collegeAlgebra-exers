\(P(x_1, y_1)\) and \(Q(x_2,y_2)\text{,}\) the distance between \(P\) and \(Q\) is
\begin{equation*} |PQ|=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2} \end{equation*}
and the coordinates of the midpoint of the segment that joins \(P\) and \(Q\) are
\begin{equation*} x_m = \frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \ \ \ \ \ y_m = \frac{y_1+y_2}{2} \end{equation*}
Therefore the distance between \((1 ,-1 )\) and \((7 ,8 )\) is
\begin{equation*} d=\sqrt{(7 - 1)^2+(8 + 1)^2} = \sqrt{117} = 10.816653826392 \end{equation*}
and the coordinates of the midpoints are
\begin{equation*} x_m = \frac{1+7}{2} = 4, \ \ \ \ \ y_m = \frac{-1+8}{2} = 3.5 \end{equation*}
Note: if you did not know the formulas for the coordinates of the midpoint, you could just draw a coordinate system, plot the given points, and find the midpoint of the segment joining them. You would see from the picture what are the coordinates of the midpoint.
