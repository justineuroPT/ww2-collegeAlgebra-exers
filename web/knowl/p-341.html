Solution: The product of a complex number and its conjugate complex equals the sum of the squares of the real and imaginary parts. Of course we can also see this directly:
\begin{equation*} (a+bi)(a-bi) = a^2 +abi-abi-b^2i^2 = a^2 + b^2 \end{equation*}
For the second part of the problem, note that
\begin{equation*} (a+bi)^2 = a^2-b^2 + 2abi. \end{equation*}
By replacing \(b\) with \(-b\) in this equation we obtain
\begin{equation*} (a+bi)^2 = a^2-b^2 - 2abi. \end{equation*}
Notice only the imaginary part of the result changes (from positive to negative). Hence
\begin{equation*} (a+bi)^2 - (a-bi)^2 = 4abi. \end{equation*}
